Who Was I?

Growing up, I wasn’t the most outgoing kid. I spent a lot of time on my own, and I often felt like I didn’t fit in. While others had groups of friends, I mostly had my own little world, and at first, I didn’t really know where I belonged. But everything changed when I discovered game development. Creating games became more than a hobby, it became an escape, a comfort, and eventually the thing that made me feel excited about the future. Building worlds, designing mechanics, and coding gave me direction when I didn’t have much else. It became the place where I felt confident, creative, and in control. Those quiet years shaped me. They taught me to be independent, to learn on my own, and to use creativity as a strength. And that’s the foundation of who I am today.

More About Myself

Who Am I?

Today, I’m someone who finds joy in creating and exploring. I’m still learning, still experimenting, and still pushing myself to grow every day. Programming and game development are not just hobbies they are how I challenge myself, express my ideas, and bring my imagination to life. I’m curious, independent, and driven by the desire to improve. Even though I’m still young and have a lot to learn, I’m proud of the projects I’ve built, the skills I’ve developed, and the passion that keeps me moving forward.

Who Do I Want To Be?

I want to become someone who continuously grows, meets my own expectations, and earns respect from myself and from others. I strive to live with discipline, integrity, and purpose. Most importantly, I want to create meaningful things projects, ideas, or innovations that can make a real difference and help change the world for the better.

My Growth Timeline

Kindergarten — Even as a quiet, loner kid, I was already fascinated by creating. I would imagine and design things entirely in my mind, exploring ideas and worlds without writing them down. That early curiosity sparked my love for making and imagining.
Elementary — This is when I started turning imagination into action. I began planning and creating simple games just for fun, experimenting with coding and game mechanics. Those first attempts taught me the joy of building something from nothing.
High School — By now, I had discovered my true passion for programming and game development. I spent more time creating, learning, and improving my skills. Even while facing challenges in life and social interactions, I found purpose and direction through my projects.
Senior High School — The journey became more serious as I began thinking about my future and career path. Choosing my path was daunting at first, but I embraced the challenge, grew more disciplined, confident, and focused, and continued building meaningful connections. Today, I’m proud of how far I’ve come, both in my skills and in my relationships.

Concluding Reflection

Looking back at my journey, I realize how much I have grown not just in skills, but in understanding myself and my passions. From a quiet, imaginative child to someone who has made many friends and found connection, my life as a loner has transformed. Each stage of my life has taught me resilience, independence, and the value of persistence. My love for game development and programming has been more than a hobby; it has been a guiding force, shaping who I am and who I aspire to be. As I continue to learn and face new challenges, I carry with me the lessons of my past, the curiosity of my present, and the ambition to make a positive impact in the future.
